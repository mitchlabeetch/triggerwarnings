Frequently Asked Questions

What trigger categories are supported? +

We support 28 trigger categories including: Violence, Gore, Blood, Sexual Assault, Sex/Nudity, Self-Harm, Suicide, Substance Abuse (Drugs/Alcohol), Murder, Torture, Domestic Violence, Child Abuse, Animal Cruelty, Vomit, Medical Procedures, Eating Disorders, Flashing Lights, Spiders/Snakes, Swear Words, Racial Violence, LGBTQ+ Phobia, Natural Disasters, Religious Trauma, Jump Scares, Cannibalism, Dead Bodies/Body Horror, Children Screaming, and Detonations/Bombs.

How does real-time subtitle analysis work? +

When you enable subtitles/captions, our extension reads the text in real-time and scans for 120+ trigger keywords. When a match is found, you get a warning 5 seconds before that moment in the video. This works on ANY content, even if it's not in our database. All processing happens locally in your browser - no data is sent to servers.

Is my viewing data collected or tracked? +

Absolutely not. We take privacy seriously. All detection happens locally in your browser. We don't collect viewing history, personal information, or track what you watch. The extension only queries our database for trigger warnings based on video IDs, and all real-time analysis (subtitle scanning, photosensitivity detection) runs entirely on your device. We never see your video content.

What is photosensitivity detection? +

Photosensitivity detection protects users with photosensitive epilepsy by automatically detecting rapid flashing in video content. Using WCAG 2.1 guidelines, we analyze video frames for luminance changes and alert you before sequences with more than 3 flashes per second. This feature is always active (you can't disable it) because it's critical for health and safety. It uses less than 2% CPU and processes everything locally.

What if a movie/show isn't in your database? +

That's the beauty of our multi-layered approach! Even if content isn't in our database:

  • Subtitle Analysis will still detect triggers if you have captions enabled
  • Photosensitivity Detection always runs, protecting against rapid flashing
  • You can submit warnings while watching to help future viewers

This means the extension provides value even for brand new releases!

Can I customize which warnings I see? +

Yes! You have complete control:

  • Category Selection: Choose which of the 28 categories you want warnings for
  • Multiple Profiles: Create different profiles for different situations (e.g., "Alone" vs "With Family")
  • Lead Time: Adjust how early you get warnings (5-60 seconds)
  • Banner Position: Choose where warnings appear (4 corners)
  • Spoiler-Free Mode: Hide exact timestamps to avoid spoilers
  • Visual Settings: Customize font size, transparency, and duration
How accurate are the warnings? +

Accuracy varies by detection method:

  • Database (DTDD): ~85% accurate - community-verified with voting
  • Database (IMDb): ~75% accurate - community-sourced
  • Subtitle Analysis: 70-90% - depends on keyword clarity
  • Photosensitivity: ~95% - algorithmic detection is very reliable

You can vote on warnings to help improve accuracy, and we use confidence scores to filter low-quality submissions.

Does this slow down my streaming? +

No! Performance impact is minimal:

  • CPU Usage: Less than 3% total (subtitle analysis <1%, photosensitivity <2%)
  • Memory: Less than 10MB
  • Network: Only initial database query (~50KB), then everything is local
  • Video Quality: No impact - we don't touch the video stream

Most users won't notice any performance difference.
